% --- Abstract ----------------------------------------------------------------
\begin{abstract}
Epidemic waves create rapid, spatially uneven pressure on critical-care
services, requiring health systems to decide how to pre-position surge
capacity and route patients under forecast uncertainty. We propose a
forecast-to-decision pipeline that couples a per-region physics-informed
neural forecaster on the refined $SEI_aI_sHCRD$ compartmental system to
a robust mixed-integer-linear allocation model. The forecaster is
trained directly on a multi-quantile pinball loss, which is decision-
aware by construction (the $q^{(0.9)}$ component penalises
under-prediction nine-to-one), and uses a learnable level-and-trend
anchor that residual-connects the most recent observation to every
horizon-quantile prediction. Quantile forecasts are discretised into
three demand scenarios that drive a regional bed-surge allocation
problem solved exactly via PuLP+CBC and approximately via a Genetic
Algorithm, the Non-dominated Sorting Genetic Algorithm II, and
Simulated Annealing on a shared LP-slave fitness oracle. On a
seven-region NHS England case study spanning $1$~August~$2020$ to
$31$~August~$2022$, the proposed forecaster reduces $14$-day RMSE by
$19\%$ over per-origin re-fitted ARIMA and the $28$-day under-
estimation rate by four-fold; the tail-risk robust MILP achieves
the lowest transfer burden in the policy set ($4.6\times$ below
population-proportional) at no cost in expected or worst-case
coverage. A four-axis sensitivity sweep (forecaster, surge budget,
CVaR weight, mutual-aid travel cap) clarifies when each control binds.

\keywords{physics-informed neural networks, decision-aware forecasting,
          pinball loss, robust optimisation, metaheuristics, NSGA-II,
          critical-care planning, NHS England}
\end{abstract}

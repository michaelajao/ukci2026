% Abstract
\begin{abstract}
NHS critical-care planning during epidemic waves must pre-position
surge capacity and route patients across regions under forecast
uncertainty. We propose a forecast-to-decision pipeline coupling a
per-region physics-informed forecaster on the $SEI_aI_sHCRD$ system
to a tail-risk robust MILP for bed-surge allocation. The forecaster is
trained on a multi-quantile pinball loss whose $q^{(0.9)}$ component
penalises under-prediction nine-to-one and uses a learnable
level-and-trend anchor. Quantile forecasts drive a hybrid-regional
allocation solved exactly via PuLP+CBC and approximately via GA,
NSGA-II, and Simulated Annealing on a shared LP-slave oracle. On a
seven-region NHS England case study (Aug~$2020$ to Aug~$2022$), the
forecaster cuts $14$-day RMSE over per-origin re-fitted ARIMA by
$19\%$ and the $28$-day underestimation rate four-fold; the robust
MILP achieves the lowest transfer burden ($4.6\times$ below
population-proportional) at no cost in coverage. A four-axis
sensitivity sweep clarifies when each control binds.

\keywords{physics-informed neural networks, decision-aware forecasting,
          pinball loss, robust optimisation, metaheuristics, NSGA-II,
          NHS England}
\end{abstract}

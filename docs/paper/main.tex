% Abstract
\begin{abstract}
NHS critical-care planning during epidemic waves must pre-position surge capacity and route patients across regions before demand is known. We propose a forecast-to-decision pipeline that couples a per-region physics-informed forecaster on the $SEI_aI_sHCRD$ system to a tail-risk robust linear program for bed-surge allocation. The forecaster is trained with a cost-asymmetric multi-quantile pinball loss whose $q^{(0.9)}$ branch penalises under-prediction nine-to-one, together with a learnable level-and-trend anchor. On a seven-region NHS England case study (Aug~$2020$ to Aug~$2022$) it cuts $14$-day RMSE by $19\%$ over per-origin re-fitted ARIMA and by $37\%$ over a neural GRU, while ARIMA stays stronger at the longest horizon, so the model is best read as a short-to-medium-horizon operational forecaster. Its quantile forecasts drive a hybrid-regional allocation solved exactly. Among budget-exhausting policies with identical coverage, the tail-risk robust formulation attains the lowest patient-transfer burden, a binding-budget routing effect rather than an unmet-demand reduction at the reported operating point. A sensitivity analysis over the forecaster, budget, tail-risk weight, and travel cap shows the budget to be the dominant control.

\keywords{physics-informed neural networks, cost-asymmetric forecasting,
          pinball loss, robust optimisation, linear programming,
          NHS England}
\end{abstract}

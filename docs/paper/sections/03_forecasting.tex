\subsection{SEIRD Compartmental Model}\label{ssec:seird}

For each region $r \in \R$ with population $N_r$ we model the local
epidemic with an eight-compartment $SEI_aI_sHCRD$ system whose states
are susceptible, exposed, asymptomatic/symptomatic infectious,
hospitalised, critical-care, recovered, and deceased. \Cref{fig:seird}
shows the flow topology, in which the distinct probabilities $\omega$
($I_s\!\to\!H$) and $\phi$ ($H\!\to\!C$) disambiguate the two clinical
thresholds. The system evolves as
\begin{equation}\label{eq:seird}
\begin{aligned}
\dot{S}_r     &= -\beta_r S_r (I_{s,r}+I_{a,r})/N_r + \eta_r R_r, \quad
\dot{E}_r      = \beta_r S_r (I_{s,r}+I_{a,r})/N_r - \alpha E_r, \\
\dot{I}_{s,r} &= \alpha\rho E_r - d_s I_{s,r}, \quad
\dot{I}_{a,r}  = \alpha(1-\rho)E_r - d_a I_{a,r}, \\
\dot{H}_r     &= d_s\omega I_{s,r} - (d_H + \mu) H_r, \quad
\dot{C}_r      = \phi\, d_H H_r - (\gamma_{c,r}+\delta_{c,r}) C_r, \\
\dot{R}_r     &= d_s(1{-}\omega)I_{s,r} + d_a I_{a,r}
                + (1{-}\phi)d_H H_r + \gamma_{c,r}C_r - \eta_r R_r, \\
\dot{D}_r     &= \mu H_r + \delta_{c,r} C_r,
\end{aligned}
\end{equation}
subject to $S_r + E_r + I_{a,r} + I_{s,r} + H_r + C_r + R_r + D_r = N_r$. Here
$\alpha, d_s, d_a, d_H, \rho, \omega, \mu, \phi$ are fixed clinical
constants and $\beta_r,\gamma_{c,r},\delta_{c,r},\eta_r$ are
time-varying region-specific parameters learned from data. The earlier
formulation in~\cite{ajao2025hybrid} overloaded $\omega$ to play both
roles; the present split makes the hospitalisation and critical-care
escalation thresholds independently identifiable.

\subsection{Per-Region PINN-SEIRD}\label{ssec:pinn}

The PINN consists of two MLPs following~\cite{ajao2025hybrid}: a state
network $U^r_{NN}$ (five hidden layers of $20$ units) and a parameter
network $X^r_{NN}$ for $(\beta_r,\gamma_{c,r},\delta_{c,r},\eta_r)$
(three hidden layers of $20$ units), with tanh hidden activations and
sigmoid outputs. The loss combines a data fit on observable compartments
$\{H,C\}$, the only ones with daily regional NHS counts, with an
ODE residual against \Cref{eq:seird}:
\begin{equation}\label{eq:pinn_loss}
\begin{aligned}
\mathcal{L}^{PINN}_r
&= \sum_{t \in \T_{\text{train}}}\sum_{k \in \{H,C\}}
  \big(U^r_{NN,k}(t) - y_{r,t,k}/N_r\big)^2 \\
&\quad + \lambda_{ode}\sum_{t \in \T_{\text{coll}}}
  \Big\|\tfrac{dU^r_{NN}(t)}{dt} -
  \mathcal{N}(U^r_{NN}; X^r_{NN})\Big\|^2,
\end{aligned}
\end{equation}
where $\mathcal{N}$ is the right-hand side of \Cref{eq:seird},
derivatives use PyTorch autograd at $256$ collocation points sampled
uniformly in $\T_{\text{coll}}$, and observations are population-
normalised. We pre-train each region's PINN for $1500$ Adam steps with
$\lambda_{ode}=0.1$, $\text{lr}=5\!\times\!10^{-3}$; both terms converge
to the $2\text{--}8\times 10^{-6}$ range across regions
(mean $\sim 4\times 10^{-6}$ for both).

\paragraph{Identifiability note.}
With only $H$ and $C$ observed at regional resolution, the four
learnable parameters $(\beta_r, \gamma_{c,r}, \delta_{c,r}, \eta_r)$
are not jointly identifiable from these series alone: $\gamma_{c,r}$
and $\delta_{c,r}$ in particular appear additively in $\dot C_r$ and
cannot be separated from the $C$ trajectory. We therefore make no
epidemiological-interpretability claim for any individual parameter
trajectory; the parameter-network output is consumed as a four-channel
learned feature vector by the downstream GRU. The operational evidence
that this bundle carries signal is the ``w/o PINN params'' ablation in
\Cref{tab:forecast_accuracy} ($h{=}14$ RMSE inflates almost two-fold,
$14.46\!\to\!27.25$); a formal structural identifiability analysis
under $\{H, C\}$ observation is deferred to future work.

\subsection{Temporal Head with Level-and-Trend Anchor}\label{ssec:temporal}

Once pre-trained the PINN is \emph{frozen}: its outputs are reused as a
physics-informed temporal embedding. For each lookback window of length
$L=28$ ending at origin $t$, the augmented feature sequence is
$\mathbf{f}_{r,\tau} = [U^r_{NN}(\tau)\,\|\,X^r_{NN}(\tau)\,\|\,
\tilde y_{r,\tau}] \in \mathbb{R}^{13}$, $\tau \in \{t-L+1,\dots,t\}$,
where $\tilde y_{r,\tau}$ is the z-scored observed target. A two-layer
GRU (hidden $128$, dropout $0.15$) decodes the final hidden state via a
per-horizon two-layer MLP (width $64$, GELU) into standardised quantile
deltas $\Delta_{r,t}^{h,q}$ for $h\in\Hset=\{7,14,21,28\}$ and
$q\in\Qset=\{0.1,0.5,0.9\}$.

A \emph{level-and-trend anchor} makes the GRU's job residual:
\begin{equation}\label{eq:anchor}
\hat z_{r,t}^{h,q} \;=\; \Delta_{r,t}^{h,q}
  \;+\; \alpha^{h,q}\, \tilde y_{r,t}
  \;+\; \gamma^{h,q}\, s_{r,t}^{(7)}\,
        \!\left(\tfrac{h}{7}\right)^{\!\!\varphi^q},
\end{equation}
where $\tilde y_{r,t}$ is the most recent z-scored observation,
$s_{r,t}^{(7)} = \tilde y_{r,t}-\tilde y_{r,t-7}$ is the one-week slope,
and $\alpha^{h,q},\gamma^{h,q},\varphi^q\in(0,1)$ are learnable scalars.
The Holt-Winters-style damping exponent $\varphi^q$ prevents
slope-extrapolation overshoot at long horizons; we initialise
$\varphi^{0.5}\!\approx\!0.88$ (near-linear) and
$\varphi^{0.1}=\varphi^{0.9}=0.5$ (square-root damping). Forecasts in
the original scale follow by inverting standardisation.

\subsection{Pinball Loss for Cost-Asymmetric Quantiles}\label{ssec:loss}

The forecaster minimises the multi-quantile pinball
loss~\cite{koenker1978regression}:
\begin{equation}\label{eq:pinball}
\mathcal{L}_{\text{pinball}} = \frac{1}{|\Qset|}\sum_{q\in\Qset}
  \mathbb{E}_{(t,h)}\!\big[\max\!\big(
    q\, e,\, (q{-}1)\, e\big)\big],\quad
    e = \tilde y_{r,t+h-1} - \hat z_{r,t}^{h,q},
\end{equation}
evaluated at all $(h,q)\in\Hset\!\times\!\Qset$. The $q=0.5$ pinball
collapses to L1 (point accuracy); the $q=0.9$ pinball penalises
under-prediction nine-to-one. We use this as a cost-asymmetric proxy for
the downstream shortage asymmetry (an under-bed risks a stranded patient;
an over-bed only wastes pre-positioned capacity)~\cite{elmachtoub2022smart};
the forecaster is not trained through the allocation solver. We report $\hat y^{0.5}$
as the point forecast and $[\hat y^{0.1},\hat y^{0.9}]$ as an $80\%$
interval.

We train for up to $800$ AdamW epochs ($\text{lr}=10^{-3}$, weight decay
$10^{-4}$, cosine annealing) on Alpha-wave lookback windows, with early
stopping (patience $80$) on Delta-wave validation pinball loss. Once
selected, the model is frozen and applied to the Omicron test period via
$\Delta t = 7$-day rolling origins, yielding $32$ origin dates.

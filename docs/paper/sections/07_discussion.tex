\paragraph{Equity versus efficiency.}
The Pareto-front analysis highlights a recurring tension in
allocation policy: the lowest-cost configuration concentrates surge
capacity in the largest-demand regions, but achieves higher
maximum-regional-shortage ratios than the equity-aware variant. The
equity-linearised constraint (\Cref{eq:equity_per_region,eq:equity_max})
shifts the front toward more uniform per-capita coverage at a modest cost
premium. This trade-off is operationally relevant: equity-blind allocation
can leave outlying regions exposed, while strict equity weighting can
under-utilise concentrated capacity. The framework gives planners a
principled way to elicit and explore that trade-off rather than burying it
in a single objective.

The forecast-value experiment shows a roughly \todo{Z}\% gap in unmet
demand between the proposed forecaster and a historical-median baseline,
and a \todo{W}\% gap to oracle forecasts. The asymmetric underestimation
penalty (\Cref{eq:under_loss}) is doing meaningful work: ablating it
raises the underestimation rate by \todo{X} percentage points
(\Cref{tab:forecast_accuracy}) without proportionally improving root mean
squared error. Better accuracy on the wrong side of the forecast
distribution still produces shortages: this is the operational point of
decision-aware training. At regional scale, deterministic and robust MILP
solve exactly in seconds; at trust scale the LP-slave metaheuristics
match within \todo{X}\% of MILP at \todo{Y}$\times$ lower runtime,
extending applicability to operational instance sizes.

\paragraph{Limitations.}
Costs $F_j, g_j, c_{ij}$ are reported in scaled units; operational use
would require commissioning-context calibration. The SEIRD model assumes
well-mixed regional populations. Travel times are approximated by
detour-scaled great-circle distances; an OSRM-derived matrix may shift
particular assignments without changing qualitative conclusions. The
framework is configured for NHS England structures; transfer to other
health systems would require re-specifying demand units, capacity
categories, and equity proxies, though the optimisation core is general.
Natural extensions include graph-based forecasting backbones, a
rolling-horizon online formulation, and a coupled budget-allocation upper
level.

\section{Regional Allocation Model}\label{sec:optimisation}
% =============================================================================

The allocation module decides how many surge beds to place in each NHS
region and how to route excess demand through inter-regional patient
transfers, given the quantile forecasts of \Cref{sec:forecasting}. We
present a deterministic MILP, a tail-risk robust extension, and three
metaheuristic comparators.

\subsection{Sets, Parameters, Decision Variables}\label{ssec:notation}

The scope is \emph{hybrid regional}: candidate sites coincide with the
seven NHS England commissioning regions, so demand nodes and capacity
sites collapse to one set $\R=\{r_1,\ldots,r_7\}$. Every region is
always ``open'' (no facility-opening binary) and per-bed surge cost is
uniform; the model decides only how many beds per region and how to
route patients. Indices: regions $r,r'\!\in\!\R$; horizons
$h\!\in\!\Hset=\{7,14,21,28\}$~days; scenarios
$s\!\in\!\Sset=\{\text{low},\text{median},\text{high}\}$ from
$q^{(0.1)},q^{(0.5)},q^{(0.9)}$ with weights
$\pi=(0.2,0.6,0.2)$, an interval-representative discretisation that
treats $q^{(0.1)}, q^{(0.5)}, q^{(0.9)}$ as centroids of the lower
$20\%$, middle $60\%$, and upper $20\%$ probability mass respectively.
Parameters: forecast demand
$d_{r,h}^s=\hat q_r^{(s)}(t_0+h)$; baseline capacity
$C_r=1.05\times$Delta-peak; per-region surge cap $K_r=0.5\,C_r$; budget
$B=0.20\sum_r C_r$; great-circle inter-centroid distance $D_{r r'}$ in
km ($\times 1.3$ road-detour); travel time
$T_{r r'}=60\,D_{r r'}/80$~min; mutual-aid cap $\tau=240$~min. Decision
variables: surge $b_{r,h}\!\in\![0,K_r]$, peak auxiliary
$\bar b_r\!\geq\!0$, transfer flows $z_{r r',h}^s\!\geq\!0$ defined only
for $T_{r r'}\!\leq\!\tau$, unmet $u_{r,h}^s\!\geq\!0$, and tail-risk
auxiliary $W\!\geq\!0$.

\subsection{Deterministic Median-Scenario MILP}\label{ssec:milp}

The deterministic model optimises the median scenario $m$ ($q^{(0.5)}$):
\begin{equation}\label{eq:deterministic_objective}
\min\;
\textstyle\sum_{r,h} u_{r,h}^{m}
\;+\;\rho\sum_{r\neq r',h} D_{r r'}\, z_{r r',h}^{m},
\end{equation}
with $\rho=10^{-3}$ a transfer tie-break weight (one bed of weekly
snapshot unmet outweighs $1000$~bed-km of transfer). Each $u_{r,h}^{m}$
is a snapshot of bed-shortfall at the weekly checkpoint $h$, so the
objective is the snapshot unmet summed over the four-week planning
horizon, not a continuous bed-day integral. Constraints:
\begin{align}
\textstyle\sum_{r':T_{r r'}\leq\tau}\! z_{r r',h}^{m} + u_{r,h}^{m}
  &\geq d_{r,h}^{m}
  && \forall r,h, \label{eq:demand}\\
\textstyle\sum_{r':T_{r' r}\leq\tau}\! z_{r' r,h}^{m}
  &\leq C_r + b_{r,h}
  && \forall r,h, \label{eq:capacity}\\
b_{r,h} \leq K_r,\quad b_{r,h} &\leq \bar b_r
  && \forall r,h, \label{eq:expansion_peak}\\
\textstyle\sum_r \bar b_r &\leq B,
  && \label{eq:budget}\\
z_{r r',h}^{m} &= 0
  && \text{if } T_{r r'}\!>\!\tau, \label{eq:travel}\\
b_{r,h},\bar b_r,z_{r r',h}^{m},u_{r,h}^{m} &\geq 0.
\end{align}
\Cref{eq:demand} ensures each region's demand is treated locally,
transferred, or recorded as unmet; \Cref{eq:capacity} caps inbound flow
at baseline plus surge; \Cref{eq:expansion_peak,eq:budget} charge each
region for its peak-horizon allocation (same physical beds serve every
horizon up to peak); \Cref{eq:travel} suppresses transfers beyond the
mutual-aid window.

\subsection{Tail-Risk Robust Objective}\label{ssec:robust}

To hedge against the high-demand scenario the robust model imposes
\Cref{eq:demand,eq:capacity} for every $s\in\Sset$ and adds the
tail-risk term $\lambda_3 W$ where $W\geq\sum_{r,h} u_{r,h}^s$ for
$s\in\Sset_{\text{tail}}=\{\text{high}\}$:
\begin{equation}\label{eq:robust_objective}
\min\;
\sum_{s\in\Sset}\!\pi_s\!\Big(
\textstyle\sum_{r,h} u_{r,h}^s + \rho \sum_{r\neq r',h} D_{r r'} z_{r r',h}^s
\Big)\;+\;\lambda_3\, W.
\end{equation}
$\lambda_3{=}0$ recovers the three-scenario risk-neutral objective;
$\lambda_3\!\to\!\infty$ yields a worst-case policy on the high
scenario. We report $\lambda_3{=}1$ throughout. The three-scenario
construction is co-monotone across regions and horizons (the high
scenario imposes $q^{(0.9)}$ jointly), so the resulting tail is
over-conservative relative to the true joint distribution; we treat
this as an operationally desirable safety margin, vindicated by the
realised-demand re-evaluation in \Cref{ssec:e5_e6}.

\subsection{Exact and Metaheuristic Solvers}\label{ssec:solvers}

The robust model has $28$ horizon-specific surge variables, $7$ peak
auxiliaries, up to $588$ transfer flows, $84$ unmet variables, and the
tail auxiliary. We compare an exact MILP, three metaheuristics, and
three heuristic baselines on the same instance.

\paragraph{Exact MILP via PuLP$+$CBC.}
Both models are solved in PuLP with bundled CBC. The deterministic
allocation is re-evaluated by an LP slave over all three scenarios to
report honest $E[u]$ and $u^{\text{worst}}$. Both runs finish in
$<\!0.1$~s.

\paragraph{Master--slave structure.}
For fixed peak surge $\mathbf{b}^\star\in\mathbb{R}_+^7$ subject to
$\sum_r b^\star_r\leq B$ and $b^\star_r\leq K_r$, the residual problem
in $(z,u)$ is an LP. All metaheuristics operate on
$\mathbf{b}^\star$, calling the LP slave once per fitness evaluation.

\paragraph{GA (single-objective).}
A pymoo Genetic Algorithm minimises $E[u]$ (plus the tie-break term)
over $\mathbf{b}^\star$: real-valued encoding, SBX crossover
($\eta_c\!=\!15$, $p_c\!=\!0.9$), polynomial mutation
($\eta_m\!=\!20$, $p_m\!=\!1/7$), tournament selection, population
$50$, $40$ generations, simplex-repair after every operator.

\paragraph{NSGA-II (three-objective).}
NSGA-II~\cite{deb2002fast} evaluates three objectives jointly:
\begin{equation}\label{eq:nsga2_obj}
f_1\!=\!\textstyle\sum_r b^\star_r,\quad
f_2\!=\!\textstyle\sum_{r,h,s}\!\pi_s u_{r,h}^{s\star},\quad
f_3\!=\!\textstyle\sum_{r\neq r',h,s}\!\pi_s D_{r r'} z_{r r',h}^{s\star},
\end{equation}
where $f_1$ is total surge beds, $f_2$ is $E[u]$, $f_3$ is transfer
burden, and $\star$-quantities come from the LP slave. We use the same
encoding, population, and generation count as the GA, and select a
\emph{representative point} from the front by L2 distance to the
normalised ideal.

\paragraph{Simulated Annealing.}
Geometric cooling ($T_0{=}1$, $\gamma{=}0.97$) over Gaussian
neighbourhoods ($\sigma{=}0.10 B$) with simplex repair on the same LP
slave; $400$ proposals.

\paragraph{Heuristic baselines.}
Three closed-form policies provide a no-optimisation floor:
\emph{population-proportional} (ONS region populations);
\emph{demand-proportional} (expected peak demand); and \emph{greedy
shortage-first} (single-bed increments to the currently most-unmet
region). Each closes $\mathbf{b}^\star$ via the same LP slave.

\section{Conclusion}\label{sec:conclusion}
% =============================================================================

A per-region PINN-GRU on the $SEI_aI_sHCRD$ system with a
decision-aware multi-quantile pinball loss cuts $14$-day RMSE over
re-fitted ARIMA by $19\%$ and the $h{=}28$ underestimation rate
four-fold; its quantile forecasts drive a hybrid-regional bed-surge
allocation in which the tail-risk robust MILP attains the lowest
transfer burden at no cost in coverage. The deterministic--robust gap
quantifies the value of $\pi_s$-weighted expectation over median-only
optimisation, and the sensitivity sweep clarifies when each control
binds. Detour-scaled travel times, well-mixed regional populations,
and beds--bed-km cost units are the principal caveats; age-stratified
pathways and other health-system topologies remain natural
extensions~\cite{shamseddin2025optimization}.

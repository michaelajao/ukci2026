\section{Conclusion}\label{sec:conclusion}
% =============================================================================

We presented a forecast-to-decision optimisation framework for NHS
critical-care surge-capacity planning. A per-region physics-informed
forecaster on the refined $SEI_aI_sHCRD$ system, trained with a
decision-aware multi-quantile pinball loss and a level-and-trend
anchor, achieves a $19\%$ RMSE reduction over re-fitted ARIMA at the
operational $14$-day horizon and a four-fold reduction in
underestimation rate at $h = 28$. Its quantile outputs drive a
hybrid-regional bed-surge allocation problem solved exactly via a
PuLP+CBC MILP and approximately via GA, NSGA-II, and Simulated
Annealing on the same LP-slave fitness oracle. On the seven-region
NHS England case study the tail-risk robust MILP achieves the
lowest transfer burden in the policy set at no cost in coverage, the
deterministic--robust gap quantifies the operational cost of
optimising the median scenario alone, and a four-axis sensitivity
sweep (forecaster, surge budget, CVaR weight, mutual-aid travel cap)
clarifies when each control binds. The pipeline is released as a set
of installable Python console commands on open NHS data, supporting
reproducibility and operational re-use.

\section{Conclusion}\label{sec:conclusion}
% =============================================================================

A per-region PINN-GRU on the $SEI_aI_sHCRD$ system with a
cost-asymmetric multi-quantile pinball loss cuts $14$-day RMSE over
re-fitted ARIMA by $19\%$ and over a per-region GRU by $37\%$, while
ARIMA remains stronger at the longest point-forecast horizon. Its
quantile forecasts drive a hybrid-regional bed-surge allocation in
which the tail-risk robust MILP attains the lowest transfer burden
among tied budget-exhausting policies. The deterministic--robust gap
quantifies the value of $\pi_s$-weighted expectation over median-only
optimisation, and the sensitivity sweep clarifies when each control
binds. Detour-scaled travel times, well-mixed regional populations,
beds--bed-km cost units, and the single realised allocation origin are
the principal caveats; age-stratified pathways, all-origin allocation
distributions, and other health-system topologies remain natural
extensions~\cite{shamseddin2025optimization}.

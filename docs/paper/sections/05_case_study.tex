¥subsection{Data and Spatial Resolution}

The case study uses three publicly available data sources covering
$1$‾August‾$2020$ to $31$‾August‾$2022$, the window during which NHS
England published daily regional admissions and bed-occupancy data:
NHS‾England COVID-19‾Hospital‾Activity (daily admissions and
mechanical-ventilation bed occupancy), NHS‾England Urgent‾and‾Emergency‾Care
Bed‾Availability (adult critical-care capacity at acute-trust level,
providing $C_{j,h}$), and ONS open geography and mid-year population
estimates (region boundaries, centroids for travel-time computation, and
population denominators $p_i$). The main forecasting and regional
optimisation experiments use NHS England's seven regions: North East and
Yorkshire, North West, Midlands, East of England, London, South East, and
South West. Trust-level experiments use a subset of acute trusts with
reported critical-care capacity. Travel times $T_{ij}$ are approximated by
great-circle distances scaled by a $1.3$ detour factor, calibrated against
OSRM driving estimates on a sample of routes.

¥subsection{Train, Validation, and Test Splits}

The data are split chronologically by epidemic wave, deliberately holding
the most clinically distinct wave in the test set: training on
$1$‾Aug‾$2020$ -- $31$‾May‾$2021$ (Alpha and early vaccination),
validation on $1$‾Jun -- $30$‾Nov‾$2021$ (Delta) for hyperparameter
tuning and early stopping, and testing on $1$‾Dec‾$2021$ -- $31$‾Aug‾$2022$
(Omicron and post-Omicron). Omicron is held out because its
case-to-hospitalisation and hospitalisation-to-critical-care ratios differ
markedly from earlier waves, providing a stress test of the framework's
generalisation.

¥subsection{Optimisation Parameters}

The fixed activation cost $F_j$ and marginal capacity cost $g_j$ are
reported in scaled units, allowing sensitivity analysis without committing
to monetary figures that depend on commissioning context. The budget $B$
is varied across three levels (low, medium, high) to expose the
cost--shortage trade-off, the travel-time threshold $¥tau$ over
$¥{60, 120, 240¥}$ minutes, and equity and CVaR weights $¥lambda_2,
¥lambda_3$ via sensitivity sweeps. Default loss weights are $¥lambda_1 =
10^3$, $¥lambda_2 = 10^2$, $¥lambda_3 = 10^1$, calibrated to bring the
four objective components to comparable magnitudes. All experiments use
fixed random seeds; results are reported as means over five seeds where
applicable. Code, configurations, and the cleaned regional dataset are
available at the repository linked in the acknowledgements.

\section{NHS England Case Study}\label{sec:case_study}
% =============================================================================

The case study uses two public sources covering $1$~Aug~$2020$ --
$31$~Aug~$2022$ (the period of daily NHS regional publication):
NHS~England COVID-19~Hospital~Activity~\cite{nhs_covid_activity}, which
provides daily admissions, total hospital cases, total occupied beds,
and mechanical-ventilation bed occupancy at NHS regional resolution; and
ONS Mid-Year Population Estimates~\cite{ons_mye_2021} on 2021 geography,
used both as the per-region PINN normaliser and as the denominator of
the population-proportional allocation baseline. All experiments run on
the seven NHS England commissioning regions (Y56--Y63), and
inter-regional travel times $T_{r r'}$ are approximated by
inter-centroid great-circle distances $\times 1.3$ detour at $80$~km/h.
The series is split chronologically by epidemic wave so the most
clinically distinct wave is held out: training on $1$~Aug~$2020$ --
$31$~May~$2021$ (Alpha and early vaccination), validation on
$1$~Jun -- $30$~Nov~$2021$ (Delta) for hyper-parameter selection and
early stopping, and testing on $1$~Dec~$2021$ -- $31$~Aug~$2022$
(Omicron and post-Omicron). Omicron's case-to-hospitalisation and
hospitalisation-to-critical-care ratios differ markedly from earlier
waves, providing a regime-shift stress test.

For the allocation problem, baseline capacity $C_r$ is each region's
Delta-wave peak MV occupancy $\times 1.05$; per-region surge cap is
$K_r=0.5\,C_r$; total budget is $B=0.20\sum_r C_r=213$~beds; and the
mutual-aid travel cap is $\tau=240$~min. The transfer tie-break weight
is $\rho=10^{-3}$, so one bed-day of unmet demand outweighs
$1000$~bed-km of transfer. Scenario weights are $\pi=(0.2,0.6,0.2)$ and
the robust CVaR weight is $\lambda_3=1$; all four are individually
swept in \Cref{ssec:e5_e6}. Forecast metrics are macro-averaged across
the seven regions and the $32$ rolling-origin evaluations spanning the
Omicron test period; allocation experiments are reported at the first
Omicron decision origin ($29$~Dec~$2021$). Code, configurations, and
the cleaned dataset are available at the repository linked in the
acknowledgements.
